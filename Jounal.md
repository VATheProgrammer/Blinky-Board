# DAY 2(7 months after)(1 hour)  
I  picked up this scrapped project and built it for Outpost. 
It miraculously somehow worked.
I taught myself how to solder using a Youtube tutorial, and caught on very quick.  
I actually soldered everything while on FaceTime with my friend as we were dicussing research opportunities together while I was working on this. He found the idea of me struggling with a soldering iron very funny.  
I figured out what part goes where using EasyEDA's built in PCB 3D visualizer, which showed me exactly what each part looked like, allowing me to copy and place the parts exactly where I needed to.  
To hold the board, I used some foam to hold the board up vertically, then stabbed all of the already attached pins in, then stabbed in the new one into the foam, which ensures it doesn't move when I solder it.  
Once done, I recorded a Youtube video of my project working, and submitted it to Outpost.  
The entire process was really fun.
